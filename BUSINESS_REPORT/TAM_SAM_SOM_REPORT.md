## TAM / SAM / SOM (EU, focus Germany) — Slide-ready content + speaker notes

### Slide 1 — TAM (Total Addressable Market, EU)

**On-slide content (keep it sparse):**
- **TAM (EU-27)**: **0.45M–1.80M evaluations / year**
- **At our B2C pricing**: **~€3M–€18M / year**
- **Sizing unit**: paid *evaluation reports/tests* (not diagnosis)

**Presenter notes (what to say):**
- **Definition**: TAM is the *maximum annual demand* for ADHD-related evaluations we could serve if we had full EU reach.
- **Method**: population-based demand model:

  `Annual evaluations = Population × ADHD-like prevalence × Annual evaluation-seeking rate`

- **Inputs (cited)**:
  - EU population (EU‑27): **450.4M** (Eurostat, 1 Jan 2025) `https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/web/products-eurostat-news/w/ddn-20250711-1`
  - Adult ADHD prevalence (strict/persistent): **~2.5%** pooled estimate (Simon et al., 2009) `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19252145/`
  - Child/adolescent prevalence varies by method; Europe-focused review reports **~2.9%** pooled estimate (high heterogeneity) `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36581685/`
- **Assumptions (explicit, conservative)**:
  - Prevalence band for “ADHD-like pool” across all ages: **2.5% / 3% / 4%** (low/base/high)
  - Annual evaluation-seeking rate: **4% / 6% / 10%** (low/base/high)
  - Revenue uses your **B2C prices** and a blended value (packs discount):
    - Blended € per evaluation: **€7.00 / €7.50 / €9.99** (low/base/high)
- **Why this is realistic**: the bottleneck in health-adjacent tools is not “how many people exist”, it’s **trust + distribution + institutional adoption**, which is handled later in SAM/SOM.


### Slide 2 — SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market, Germany-first)

**On-slide content:**
- **SAM (Germany demand)**: **~0.084M–0.334M evaluations / year**
- **Germany focus**: distribution + language + procurement reality
- **B2B priority segments (Germany)**:
  - **Outpatient psychotherapeutic workforce**: **34,921** (KBV, 2024)
  - **Higher-ed institutions**: **422** (WS 2024/25)

**Presenter notes:**
- **Definition**: SAM is the part of TAM we can realistically serve with our initial geography + channels (Germany-first).
- **Germany demand model** (same formula as TAM, but Germany population):
  - Germany population: **83.6M** (Destatis, end of 2024) `https://www.destatis.de/DE/Presse/Pressemitteilungen/2025/06/PD25_221_124.html`
- **B2B priority rationale**: institutions already run structured workflows and can deploy under oversight; B2C-only is harder due to trust/liability and high acquisition costs.
- **B2B “addressable accounts” framing** (conservative, because not every professional/org will buy):
  - KBV shows **34,921** psychotherapeutic professionals in outpatient contracted care (as of 31 Dec 2024) `https://www.kbv.de/infothek/zahlen-und-fakten/gesundheitsdaten/fachgruppe-und-schwerpunkt`
  - CHE Hochschuldaten lists **422** German higher-ed institutions (WS 2024/25) `https://hochschuldaten.che.de/deutschland/hochschulen/`
  - We treat these as the **starting universe** for clinical + university counseling adoption, then apply low penetration in SOM.
- **Optional supporting context (if asked)**:
  - Germany pupils total (system-level scale): **~11.249M** (Destatis, 2023/24) `https://www.destatis.de/DE/Presse/Pressemitteilungen/2024/03/PD24_101_211.html`
  - Use this to show that institutional settings are “high-throughput”, but keep “schools” out of the core SAM unless you have a clean non-paywalled school-count citation.


### Slide 3 — SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market, 36 months, Germany)

**On-slide content:**
- **36-month SOM target (Germany)**:
  - **B2B**: **30–120 paying accounts** (end of month 36)
  - **B2C**: **5k–30k paid evaluations** (cumulative over 36 months)
- **Month-36 B2B run-rate**: **~€82k–€330k ARR** (depending on plan mix)

**Presenter notes:**
- **Definition**: SOM is what we can *actually win* in 36 months given adoption cycles, sales capacity, and credibility constraints.
- **Why low penetration is realistic**:
  - Health-adjacent evaluation tooling faces **validation requirements**, **workflow friction**, and **procurement delays**.
  - So we forecast **low single-digit % penetration** of addressable B2B accounts within 36 months.
- **How the B2B run-rate is computed** (uses your posted plan prices; show the mix only if asked):
  - Low case example mix (30 accounts): 20× Basic + 8× Business + 2× Pro → **~€6.8k MRR** → **~€82k ARR run-rate**
  - Base case example mix (60 accounts): 40× Basic + 15× Business + 5× Pro → **~€13.75k MRR** → **~€165k ARR run-rate**
  - High case example mix (120 accounts): 80× Basic + 30× Business + 10× Pro → **~€27.5k MRR** → **~€330k ARR run-rate**
- **B2C volume realism**:
  - Your B2C price is low, so constraint is **distribution + trust**, not willingness-to-pay.
  - We model cumulative paid evaluations over 36 months, then multiply by blended € per eval for revenue.
